# フランス革命が勃発した3つの理由【COTEN RADIO #48】

## 要約 (NotebookLM AI要約)

The French Revolution was sparked by a volatile confluence of **economic ruin, ideological shifts, and rigid social hierarchies** that could no longer contain modern pressures. Decades of expensive warfare led to a **total fiscal collapse**, creating a massive economic disparity between a struggling France and a more industrially advanced Britain. This financial crisis coincided with the rise of **Enlightenment thought**, a movement triggered by global exploration that challenged traditional Christian dogma and encouraged citizens to **reevaluate the legitimacy of absolute monarchy**. Within this environment, a new class of wealthy commoners known as the **bourgeoisie** grew frustrated by the **tax exemptions and feudal privileges** held by the nobility, while the impoverished masses simply revolted out of a desperate need for food. Ultimately, the revolution was a chaotic collision of **four distinct interest groups**—the king, the aristocrats, the wealthy merchants, and the starving peasants—whose competing demands created a unique explosion of political change that permanently altered the course of world history.

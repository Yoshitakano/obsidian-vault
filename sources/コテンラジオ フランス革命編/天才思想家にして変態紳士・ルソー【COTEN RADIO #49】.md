# 天才思想家にして変態紳士・ルソー【COTEN RADIO #49】

## 要約 (NotebookLM AI要約)

This transcript explores the contradictory life of the philosopher Jean-Jacques Rousseau, characterizing him as both a **perverted eccentric** and a **genius thinker** whose ideas laid the foundation for modern human rights. The discussion highlights the extreme gap between Rousseau's **shameful private behavior**, such as public exposure and abandoning his five children, and the profound social value of his masterpiece, **The Social Contract**. By examining how such an unstable individual became the **spiritual father of the French Revolution**, the speakers suggest that human character is rarely one-dimensional or purely virtuous. Ultimately, the text serves as a meditation on the **complexity of historical legacy**, arguing that a person's flaws and their contributions to humanity are often inseparable, **transcending simple labels of good and evil**.

# ルイ16世とマリー・アントワネット、ギロチンに散る【COTEN RADIO #53】

## 要約 (NotebookLM AI要約)

During the French Revolution, the **Prussian retreat at the Battle of Valmy** served as a psychological turning point, emboldening the revolutionaries and fostering a new sense of **nationalist confidence** within their citizen army. In the political vacuum that followed, radical figures like **Saint-Just** successfully argued that the mere existence of a king was an **insult to equality**, leading to Louis XVI's execution by a **one-vote margin**. Despite their earlier reputations for indecisiveness and vanity, both **Louis XVI and Marie Antoinette** reportedly displayed remarkable **stoicism and character growth** while facing their demise, with the King even ironically having improved the design of the **guillotine** that ended his life. Ultimately, this narrative illustrates how the **butterfly effect of a single vote** and the extreme pressures of the era transformed flawed monarchs into tragic, dignified figures.

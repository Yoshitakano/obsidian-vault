# フランス革命 ― 地に堕ちた王室への信頼【COTEN RADIO #52】

## 要約 (NotebookLM AI要約)

This transcript examines the collapse of public trust in the French monarchy, specifically highlighting how **King Louis XVI and Marie Antoinette's attempt to flee** to Austria destroyed their remaining religious and political legitimacy. While the revolutionary government initially sought a **constitutional monarchy**, the royal family's perceived betrayal led to internal factions and a disastrous **declaration of war** against Austria, which the King secretly hoped would fail so his power could be restored. The narrative centers on the rise of **Robespierre**, who correctly predicted military failure due to internal sabotage and **royal espionage**, eventually channeling populist anger to overthrow the crown. Ultimately, the text illustrates how the **Varennes Flight** and the King's repeated use of his **veto power** alienated the masses, leading to the violent transition from a monarchy to a **republican government**.

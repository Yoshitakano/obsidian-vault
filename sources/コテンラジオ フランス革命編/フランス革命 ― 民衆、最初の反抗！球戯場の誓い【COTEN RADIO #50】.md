# フランス革命 ― 民衆、最初の反抗！球戯場の誓い【COTEN RADIO #50】

## 要約 (NotebookLM AI要約)

This narrative explores the fiscal and social collapse of pre-revolutionary France, where a **bankrupt monarchy** under Louis XVI was crippled by massive debts and a failed trade treaty with England. While royal ministers attempted to stabilize the economy by taxing the **privileged classes**, the nobility repeatedly blocked these reforms to protect their own **vested interests** and tax-exempt status. This stalemate led to the fateful convening of the **Estates-General**, an ancient assembly where the nobility hoped to manipulate the voting system to maintain their dominance over the commoners. However, the Third Estate, fueled by **Enlightenment ideals** and a newfound political consciousness, refused to be silenced and famously formed the **National Assembly** at an indoor tennis court. This shift from financial dispute to **ideological revolution** marked the moment when the masses ceased being subjects and began demanding their rights as the sovereign heart of the nation.
